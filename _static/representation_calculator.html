What is Seat Equivalent Representation (SER)?

The COTHROM framework introduces Seat Equivalent Representation (SER), which directly shows how many TDs a constituency deserves based on its population.

SER = Constituency Population Ã· National Ratio

For example, if a constituency has 150,000 people and the National Ratio is 30,000 people per TD, then:

The alternative formula is more symmetric - constituencies that are +5% over and -5% under the ideal both experience the same degree of voter misrepresentation. The traditional formula creates an asymmetry where under-represented voters experience greater inequality than the percentage suggests.
